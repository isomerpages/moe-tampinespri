<h1><strong>Chinese Language</strong></h1>
<h3><strong>Chinese Language Curriculum</strong></h3>
<p>Our Chinese Language curriculum is thoughtfully designed to nurture confident
communicators, critical thinkers, and culturally rooted learners. Through
a balanced approach that integrates language skills, cultural appreciation,
and authentic learning experiences, we aim to cultivate students’ love
for the Chinese language while equipping them with 21st century competencies.</p>
<h4><strong>Enrichment Programmes and Cultural Experiences</strong></h4>
<p>We believe that language learning extends beyond the classroom. Our students
participate in a wide range of enrichment programmes to deepen their appreciation
of Chinese culture and heritage.</p>
<p>The Cultural Performance Exposure Scheme (CPES) provides students with
opportunities to experience Chinese culture in authentic settings, allowing
them to appreciate traditions within an immersive learning environment.</p>
<p>The annual Mother Tongue Fortnight is a vibrant celebration of language
and culture. Students engage in craft workshops, cultural games, and experiential
activities that make language learning lively and relevant.</p>
<p>Our Chinese New Year celebration offers authentic opportunities for students
to experience festive customs and values. Through performances and VIA
initiatives such as Project Hongbao, students learn the spirit of compassion,
gratitude, and generosity while strengthening their cultural identity.</p>

<h4><strong>Teaching and Learning</strong></h4>
<p>Our curriculum adopts engaging, student-centred pedagogies to build strong
language foundations. Through role-play, drama, group discussions, and
collaborative tasks, students construct knowledge and apply language skills
in meaningful contexts. We also harness technology to enhance language
acquisition, using digital platforms to actively involve students.</p>
<p>Well-designed learning packages provide structured support for reading,
oral communication, and writing skills. Differentiated materials address
diverse learning needs, ensuring that every student can progress with confidence.</p>

<h4><strong>Promoting the Culture of Reading</strong></h4>
<p>Students are subscribed to Joy Reader, which provides engaging reading
materials tailored to their proficiency levels. This supports vocabulary
development, comprehension skills, and the cultivation of sustained reading
habits.</p>
<p>During the weekly reading period, activities such as mass reading, teacher-guided
reading, book sharing, and reading reflections are conducted to nurture
students’ love for reading.</p>
